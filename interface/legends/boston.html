Problem: boston housing

The goal is to estimate the median value of owner-occupied homes using the following variables (note: all variables are normalized):

  • \( x_0= \) Per capita crime rate by town
  • \( x_1= \) Proportion of residential land zoned for lots over 25,000 sq.ft.
  • \( x_2= \) Proportion of non-retail business acres per town.
  • \( x_3= \) Charles River dummy variable (1 if tract bounds river; 0 otherwise)
  • \( x_4= \) Nitric oxides concentration (parts per 10 million)
  • \( x_5= \) Average number of rooms per dwelling
  • \( x_6= \) Proportion of owner-occupied units built prior to 1940
  • \( x_7= \) Weighted distances to five Boston employment centres
  • \( x_8= \) Index of accessibility to radial highways
  • \( x_9= \) Full-value property-tax rate per $10,000
  • \( x_{10}= \) Pupil-teacher ratio by town
  • \( x_{11}= \) Index proportional to black people by town (potential unfairness or racism issue?)
  • \( x_{12}= \) % lower status of the population
